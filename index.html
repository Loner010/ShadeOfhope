Common Questions About Online Counselling

Is online counselling as effective as in-person?

Research shows online counselling is equally effective for most concerns. You receive the same professional care, therapeutic techniques, and personal attention from the comfort of your chosen space.

What technology do I need?

Just a smartphone, tablet, or computer with internet connection. We use secure, user-friendly platforms and provide technical support to ensure smooth sessions.

How does medical aid billing work?

We provide detailed invoices with our practice number (1261444) that you submit to your medical aid. Most schemes recognize online counselling and reimburse from your benefits.

Is my privacy protected online?

Absolutely. We use encrypted, HIPAA-compliant platforms designed for healthcare. Your sessions are as private and confidential as in-person meetings.

What if I have technical difficulties?

We provide pre-session technical checks and immediate support during sessions. If issues arise, we can switch to phone or reschedule at no charge.

Can I switch between online and in-person?

While we specialize in online sessions, we can discuss hybrid approaches during your consultation to find what works best for your healing journey.

Common Questions About Psychology & Counselling

Let's address common questions and clear up misconceptions about psychology, therapy, and mental health support.

Common Misconceptions

"Only 'crazy' people need therapy"

Truth: Therapy is for anyone facing life's challenges. Most clients are everyday people dealing with stress, relationships, career changes, grief, or personal growth. Seeking help shows strength and self-awareness, not weakness. Just like we see doctors for physical health, psychologists help with mental and emotional wellbeing.

"Therapy is just talking - I can talk to friends"

Truth: While friends provide valuable support, therapy offers professional care, evidence-based techniques, and an unbiased perspective. Counsellors are trained to identify patterns, provide coping strategies, and guide you through structured healing processes that friends, despite their good intentions, cannot provide.

"Therapy takes years and costs a fortune"

Truth: Many people see improvements in just a few sessions. The length depends on your goals and situation. With medical aid coverage, therapy is often more affordable than people think. Online sessions also reduce travel costs and time, making therapy more accessible and convenient.

"I should be able to handle this myself"

Truth: We all need support sometimes. You wouldn't expect to fix a broken bone without medical help - mental health deserves the same professional care. Seeking therapy shows wisdom and courage. It's about learning tools and gaining insights that help you handle future challenges more effectively.

"Online therapy isn't 'real' therapy"

Truth: Research consistently shows online therapy is as effective as in-person sessions for most concerns. You receive the same professional training, therapeutic techniques, and personalized care. Many clients prefer the comfort and privacy of their own space, leading to more open and honest conversations.

Practical Questions

How do I know if I need therapy?

Consider therapy if you're experiencing persistent sadness, anxiety, relationship difficulties, major life changes, trauma, addiction issues, or feeling stuck. If daily activities become challenging or you're not coping as well as usual, professional support can help. You don't need to wait for a crisis - therapy is also valuable for personal growth and prevention.

What's the difference between psychologists, psychiatrists, and counsellors?

Psychologists have advanced degrees in psychology and provide therapy and assessments. Psychiatrists are medical doctors who can prescribe medication and often work with severe mental illness. Registered Counsellors (like myself) have specialized training in counselling and therapy, focusing on talk therapy and behavioral interventions. All are valuable - the choice depends on your specific needs.

Will my information be kept confidential?

Absolutely. Confidentiality is a cornerstone of therapy and protected by law. Information is only shared with your written consent, except in rare circumstances involving imminent danger to yourself or others, or suspected child abuse. Even then, you'll be informed whenever possible. Your privacy and trust are sacred to the therapeutic process.

How long does therapy typically take?

This varies greatly depending on your goals and situation. Some people feel better after just a few sessions, while others benefit from longer-term support. We'll regularly review your progress and adjust the frequency as needed. Many clients start weekly, then move to bi-weekly or monthly sessions as they improve. You're always in control of the pace.

What if I don't feel comfortable with my therapist?

The therapeutic relationship is crucial for success. If you don't feel comfortable, please discuss this openly - sometimes these conversations lead to breakthroughs. If the fit still isn't right, it's perfectly acceptable to seek another therapist. I can help you find someone who better matches your needs. Your comfort and progress are what matter most.
